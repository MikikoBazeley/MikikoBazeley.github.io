SFDC/CRM Onboarding:
Establishing a new process

The next step in automating and enabling your salesforce....

Team Details:
During the implementation process at a pervious company, the team that composed of myself, my Operations manager, the IT team and the executive leadership realized we had no clear process for rolling users into a new Salesforce instance that was years from being the perfect fit for a growing company.

While mapping the various business processes we realized that there were disparate but powerful processes throughout the departments and product lines that needed to be respected but eventually reconciled in a consistent, scaleable structure.

Goals:
Have an established process of onboarding rolled out to priority users (that could then be scaled to multiple teams, divisions, and organizations).

Tools Used:

  • * Salesforce
  • * Asana: for tracking stepsand follow-ups (however this could also be managed and tracked in Salesforce using apps from the AppExchange)

For examples of how we used Asana and created tasks to ensure the user process, please see the end of the page.

Results:
By the end of the process, the individual users had a deep understanding of: limitations and usage for systems and tools, the channels for which they can report bugs and suggestions, and the resources available to learn more.

There was also increased buy-in by users critical to adoption and a change in the dynamic and understanding of the Admin as a shared resource.

Ultimately the most important result was the effect our additional information about the needs of the sales, marketing and product management teams had on informing the implementation road map and increasing sales reporting accuracy.

Deliverables

(see sidebar)

  • - Complete user packet listing the onboarding process with a checklist
  • - User-Admin agreement template
  • - Established tracking system for user onboarding process, with completed and remaining stages clearly delineated
  • - User folders compiled listing the user's needs, security/permissions, and workflow.
  • - Compiled list of plug-ins that enhanced the user experience (including Salesforce1, Brisk.io,etc)
  • - A Salesforce instance that is customized to each user's needs and preferences without loss of core priority features and workflows.

Asana for Salesforce project management

Since Asana had already been widely adopted and was the task management tool of choice for the team, it seemed like an obvious fit for use.
