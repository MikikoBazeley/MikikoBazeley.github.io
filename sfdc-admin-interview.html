SFDC/CRM Hiring:
Lead the technical interview

Find out whether your candidate is SFDC ready....

Project Details:
At a previous position I lead the technical interviews for the first Salesforce admin for the company, including writing and managing the sandbox SFDC instance.

The company had traditionally utilized Microsoft products, including Dynamics CRM. And when the business units had SFDC, the admin duties had been passed around until everyone in the company had admin level security settings. As the business grew it soon became clear that we needed a scaleabl solution that included tighter integration with marketing as well as a resource devoted solely to administering and developing the CRM with the business. As a Salesforce power user and admin for a previous start-up I was asked to lead the interviews to find a Salesforce admin.

As prevalent as Salesforce is there is very little material on how to evaluate an individualâ€™s capabilities aside from the certifications that may or may not signal experience and skill (for example, we had a salesforce developer that made it through three rounds of interviews before we discovered they were unable to write a single for-loop).

I researched and wrote original documentation such as a case study involving a company called Asimov Enterprise and their new endeavor littleBots, created and managed a sandbox organization and recorded the feedback for each candidate after their assessment, eventually presenting my recommendations to the IT team.

Goals:
Hire a SFDC aministrator with the capabilities to grow and scale the instance to meet current and future challenges.
Develop interview materials that could be used to interview remote candidates remotely and establish a baseline for fairly assessing candidates.

Tools Used:

  • * Salesforce
  • * Goto Meeting (any screensharing application will work)
  • * Timer

Results:
An interview process that vetted a candidates familiarity with the SFDC platform but also provided a fair assessment that takes into account any disruptions that could have occurred, a feedback session that encouraged candidates to analyze their problem solving strategies, and increased interest of candidates into the unique challenges our business faced.

As a result of the intense interview process, the company hired its first official SFDC admin and is in the process of developing the SFDC instance into a company wide business enablement solution.
